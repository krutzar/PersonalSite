Creative Problem Solving

Marketing Strategy

After the company graduated from Bearcat Launchpad I was responsible for executing a pre-launch marketing strategy in an attempt to gather attention and beta testers. While the company never really went anywhere in terms of launching a beta, I still learned about some of the ins and outs of marketing an early stage startup.

My Role



Born from the idea that personal technology was becoming an increasing distraction in today's society I joined eVolve as one of the original founders and was the business mind at the company. Early on the team focused on customer interviews, market research, and product iteration. If we weren't properly solving a problem for a target market, we didn't have a product. Aside from the typical product iteration work I was in charge of anything marketing related.

About eVolve



In the workplace billions are lost every year due to personal productivity, and the number one productivity killer is personal technology. eVolve looked to address this problem by creating a workplace productivity platform that not only tracked productivity and distractions across all devices, but also provided a "smart filter" that when turned on would filter out any non-essential distractions.

As we discovered near the end of the company's run, we had painted ourselves into a corner by focusing on our product and looking for a solution that matched it. This left us with something we thought was neat but companies didn't really care about. Shortly after we had realized this we decided to put eVolve on indefinite hiatus and move on to other ventures.
